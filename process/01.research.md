# 1. Introduction
This project uses biological principles as an experimental artistic methodology, translating cellular growth, drifting, attraction, repulsion, fusion, and symbiosis into visual language, interaction design, and computational logic. Through collective participation, individual data is transformed into synthetic digital organisms gradually forming an evolving digital ecosystem.


# 2. WIP Exhibition Feedback & Reflection
After presenting the first version of the project in the WIP show, I received feedback that the face-based visual output was too literal and that the interaction was too short. At that stage, the system used facial capture to generate a visual response, but the connection between the participant and the digital ecosystem needed to be more abstract and more meaningful.

In response to this feedback, I decided to revise the code and interaction design. The face would no longer appear directly in the final visual output. Instead, facial data would be analysed and transformed into abstract cellular features such as shape, colour, transparency, size, and similarity. I also decided to add voice input to influence each cell’s behaviour, and hand/touch interaction to affect the movement and gathering of cells inside the virtual petri dish.

This process allowed the project to develop from a face-based interactive sketch into a more complex digital ecosystem based on biological principles, collective participation, and computational behaviour.


# 3. Research
## Research Frame
The central question of this research is:
How can biological principles be translated into computational logic to construct an evolving digital ecosystem?

Rather than using biology solely as visual inspiration, biological organisation as a conceptual model for computational systems. Biological principles are translated into interaction models, behavioural rules and algorithmic structures that enable communication, collective organisation and emergence within a shared digital environment.

## Cell
The cell is adopted as the project's primary conceptual model because it embodies both shared organisational principles and individual variation. While all cells share a common biological structure, each possesses unique internal characteristics and behaviours, allowing individuality to exist within a collective living system.

Rather than using the cell simply as a biological image, the project translates its organisational principles—including communication, adaptation, cooperation and continuous transformation—into computational logic. These biological principles are translated into computational rules governing the generation, behaviour and relationships of synthetic digital organisms, enabling a continuously evolving computational ecosystem.

## Biological principles → Computational Ecosystem
Translation → Communication → Symbiosis → Emergence

Translation: Biological data are translated into unique synthetic cells.
Face → cell morphology
Voice → behavioural identity

Communication: Cells recognise one another through similarity analysis and exchange behavioural signals.
- Morphological similarity
- Behavioural synchronisation
- Recognition

Symbiosis: Similar cells gradually attract, communicate and merge to form collective structures.
- Attraction
- Fusion
- Collective organisation

Emergence: Continuous participation reorganises the ecosystem, producing unpredictable collective behaviours.
- Self-organisation
- Dynamic ecosystem
- Open-ended evolution
