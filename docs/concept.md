# Project Name
# Communio: Welcome To Symbiosis

# Overview
Communio is an interactive installation that explores the relationship between human beings and computational systems through the metaphor of a living digital ecosystem.
Each participant contributes a unique digital organism to a shared virtual petri dish. A camera captures a stable portrait of a single participant and translates their facial data into an individual synthetic cell. Rather than reproducing a realistic human image, the system reconstructs the face as an abstract biological form that exists between organism and computation.
After the face has been captured, the participant interacts with the installation through their voice. The characteristics of the recorded sound—including volume, rhythm and frequency—are analysed to influence the behaviour of the newly generated cell. Every voice produces a different response, giving each digital organism its own behavioural identity.

# Concept
Artificial intelligence systems increasingly rely on human-generated data. Every photograph, voice recording, behavioural pattern and online interaction contributes to the continuous training and evolution of computational systems. Participation is often voluntary, passive, conscious, or unconscious, yet every contribution becomes part of a much larger collective process.

Communio visualises this invisible relationship. Instead of presenting data as numbers or surveillance imagery, the installation transforms human biological information into synthetic life. Each participant contributes a unique computational organism that continues to exist after they leave. Rather than remaining isolated, these organisms coexist with those created by previous participants, forming an evolving digital ecology whose future cannot be fully predicted.

The title *communio* is derived from the Latin word meaning “sharing, participation, and forming a community”. It reflects the central idea of the project: every participant contributes part of themselves to a larger system. Individual data no longer belongs solely to one person but becomes part of a collective computational environment.

The subtitle "welcome to symbiosis" invites participants to enter this shared ecosystem, where human and computational agents continuously influence one another. The installation does not ask what artificial intelligence is becoming, but rather what new forms of collective life may emerge when countless human contributions are continuously synthesised into computational systems.
